# Startup Org Structure (Seed to Series B)

A reference for what a typical venture-backed startup org chart looks like at different stages. Useful for drawing org charts, planning hiring, or showing reporting lines.

## Seed (5–15 people)

Almost no formal hierarchy. Two or three founders at the top, everyone else reports directly to a founder. Common shape:

- **CEO** (founder) — sales, fundraising, recruiting
- **CTO** (founder) — engineering, product, ops
- 3–8 engineers reporting to CTO
- 1–2 generalists reporting to CEO (could be designer, growth, ops, customer success — usually one person wearing all those hats)

There are no managers. Everyone is an individual contributor. The CEO and CTO are also individual contributors most of the day.

## Seed extended / pre-Series A (15–30 people)

First specialization. The flat structure starts to break.

- **CEO**
  - VP Sales (or Head of Sales) — first sales hire who manages 1–2 AEs
  - Head of Marketing (often part-time or contract initially)
  - Head of Operations / People (one person doing finance, HR, recruiting)
- **CTO**
  - Engineering Manager (or Tech Lead) — manages 4–6 engineers
  - Head of Product (sometimes still the CEO or CTO)
  - Designer (1–2)
- **Head of Customer Success** — usually reports to CEO at this stage

The CTO probably stops writing code regularly here. The CEO stops doing direct sales.

## Series A (30–80 people)

A real management layer appears. Typical structure:

- **CEO**
  - **VP Engineering** (or CTO if they want to manage)
    - Engineering managers (2–3), each managing 5–8 engineers
    - QA / Test lead
    - DevOps / Platform team lead
  - **VP Product**
    - Product managers (2–3)
    - Design lead → designers (2–4)
  - **VP Sales**
    - Sales managers → AEs and SDRs
    - Sales engineering / solutions
  - **VP Marketing** → demand gen, content, brand
  - **Head of Customer Success** → CS managers
  - **Head of Finance** (controller, not yet CFO)
  - **Head of People**

CTO either becomes the VP Eng or steps into a pure technical role (e.g., chief architect).

## Series B (80–200 people)

Functional VPs in every area, with managers reporting to them and individual contributors below that. Three layers below CEO is common (VP → Director → Manager → IC). CFO replaces head of finance. Legal and security may become their own functions.

## Drawing tips

Org charts read top-down. CEO at the top. Direct reports as a horizontal row underneath. Each report's team hangs below them. Use solid lines for direct reporting, dotted lines for matrix relationships (e.g., a designer who sits in a product team but reports to the design lead).
